Breaking down the barriers

Many of us have something in our lifestyles that we will say prevent us from being active. Work, children, pets, energy levels, money, confidence, the weather... they are all 'reasons' or ‘excuses’ not to get moving, depending on how you look at them.

In order to break down these barriers to movement, there is a lot that we need to do as individuals, starting with being honest with ourselves as to whether these barriers are real or perceived. For the most part, they are perceived and can be overcome! Finding your ‘why’ will also help you start – and stick at – activity.

There is also a lot that needs to be done in society to encourage and assist people. Opportunities exist through initiatives from the NHS, local councils, schools, charities, businesses, and employers to make it easy for people to access these opportunities to improve their own health. Easily accessible activity that can be done to suit your lifestyle removes one major barrier.

Which is exactly what DoingOurBit does for NHS and social care workers.

Safeguarding your health

It all started when I was working in the NHS during the pandemic and realised how tough it was for my colleagues on the front line, trying to care for others while safeguarding their own well-being. It was clear we needed to look after the health of those who are ultimately going to treat us.

Covid aside, over the last few years, sickness levels in the workplace, and specifically in the NHS, have peaked and it is costing the economy billions. Waiting lists in the NHS are being affected by the highest absence levels ever where 27 million sick days are recorded in a year, losing the equivalent of 75,000 staff. The knock-on effect of this is that those working in other industries are unable to get the treatment they need to return to work, impacting not only the economy but the lives of millions of families every year.

Of course, there is a lot more to unpick with the UK health system than just sickness, but the idea behind DoingOurBit was to help with this one area; to do our bit to help keep the NHS and social care workers active and help them improve their physical and mental health, so that they, in turn, can help us all.

We really hope that more providers and fitness professionals come on board to take DoingOurBit to the next level and offer our amazing health and social care workers a free, easy to access opportunity to get active and keep healthy.

Get on board

More importantly, we hope that our health and social care staff take some time to snap up this opportunity, move more and develop some healthy habits. We have removed as many barriers as we can:

  • There is no cost
  • There is something for all fitness and ability levels
  • You can do the sessions in your own time
  • You don't need equipment
  • It doesn't matter about the weather
  • You can exercise alone or with friends and family

So please do get involved: if you’ve subscribed already make sure you set aside time each week to enjoy a few DoingOurBit sessions. And please tell your work colleagues and encourage them to sign up!

If you haven’t already signed up, give it a go. There is so much choice, you’re bound to find something you like and with new sessions being added each month, you’ll never get bored.

The DoingOurBit App is a free to participate 24-7, one-stop shop for fitness, health & wellness by the NHS, for NHS and Social Care workers.
